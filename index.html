Mérida

"La ciudad blanca"

Brief History

Yucatán is one of the three states that make up the Yucatán Peninsula. To the north is the Gulf of Mexico, which has provided lucrative trade with the United States, Europe and the Caribbean islands. The capital, Mérida, known as the white city, has a historic center dominated by Spanish colonial buildings, having been founded by Spanish conquistador Francisco de Montejo y León in 1542. The city was built to protect the Spaniards and Creoles from the revolts organized by the Maya Indians. In the late 19th century, Mérida began to prosper in the production of henequen and in the early 20th century it housed more millionaires than any other city in the world.

Delicious food

Papadzules is a traditional Yucatán dish similar to Swiss enchiladas. They are boiled egg tacos covered with pepita sauce that has a green color and red tomato sauce. The pepita sauce is obtained by roasting the pumpkin seeds, then grinding them to form a kind of powder and finally boiling the powder in water with epazote. The tomato sauce is obtained by blending tomatoes with water and boiling the mixture with a little salt.
